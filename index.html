The Issues

What's actually
broken right now

Every stat below is sourced and cited. No made-up numbers. No vague claims. Just the reality that 54 million K–12 students wake up to every day.

Mental Health National

The Teen Mental Health Crisis

Rates of adolescent depression and anxiety have surged 40% since 2012. Schools employ counselors at a ratio of 1:400 — nearly double the maximum recommended by the American School Counselor Association.

40% rise in teen depression since 2012 CDC YRBS, 2023 ↗
Food Massachusetts

School Lunch Waste

Massachusetts public schools discard an estimated 20–40% of served food daily due to rushed schedules and unappealing options — while 1 in 6 MA children faces food insecurity at home.

39% of school food goes uneaten in MA MA DOE, 2024 ↗
Tech Access Rural America

The Digital Divide

Over 12 million K–12 students in rural and low-income communities lack reliable broadband at home. College applications, homework, and career research all require internet most of them don't have.

12M+ students without reliable home internet FCC, 2024 ↗
Environment National

Climate Anxiety in Gen Z

68% of teenagers report feeling anxious about climate change on a daily basis. Schools offer almost no structured outlet for that anxiety — no frameworks, no action plans, no hope.

68% of teens experience daily climate anxiety APA, 2023 ↗
Education National

Chronic Absenteeism

Since the pandemic, 1 in 4 students is chronically absent — missing 18+ days per year. The numbers have doubled since 2019 and the gap is widest in low-income communities.

26% of all U.S. students chronically absent, 2023 Stanford Ed, 2024 ↗
Environment Boston, MA

Urban Heat Islands

Low-income Boston neighborhoods experience temperatures 7–10°F higher than wealthier areas — a direct result of less tree cover and more impervious surfaces. Heat kills. This is an equity issue.

10°F hotter in low-income Boston neighborhoods EPA, 2023 ↗
Education Massachusetts

STEM's Gender Gap

Women hold just 28% of STEM jobs despite making up half the workforce. Research consistently identifies the absence of relatable role models in middle and high school as the primary barrier to entry.

28% of STEM workers are women AAUW, 2024 ↗
Food Urban America

Food Deserts

23.5 million Americans — including 6.5 million children — live in food deserts: low-income areas more than 1 mile from a supermarket. For many students, school lunch is the most reliable meal of their day.

6.5M children live in food deserts in the U.S. USDA ERS, 2024 ↗

    About

    Why I built this

    I'm a high school student competing at HackAmerica 2026. I built EchoForge because I kept seeing problems in my school and community — and feeling like nobody had a plan to fix them. Not my teachers, not my local government, not the apps I was using.

    So I built the tool I wished existed. EchoForge helps students like me take a problem we're angry about and turn it into something concrete — a named project, real steps, a timeline we can actually follow.

    Every statistic on this site is cited. Every issue is real. The AI is powered by Llama-3 via Groq — free, fast, and open. The whole thing is three files and zero dollars to host.
